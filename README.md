COMPANY OVERVIEW

Amazon.com, Inc. is one of the world’s largest e-commerce, cloud computing, and logistics companies. The company operates across multiple business areas, including online retail, Amazon Web Services, digital advertising, subscription services, fulfillment, and transportation. At peak periods, Amazon fulfills approximately 8 million packages per day. A major part of Amazon’s competitive advantage is its ability to deliver products quickly and reliably to customers. In its 2024 annual report, Amazon reported total revenue growth from $575 billion to $638 billion, showing the scale and continued expansion of its business operations. Amazon’s logistics network operates more than 1,100 fulfillment, sortation, and delivery station facilities globally; a fleet of over 100 Amazon Air cargo aircraft; more than 30,000 branded delivery vans; and a network of over 200,000 Amazon Flex independent contractors and Delivery Service Partner (DSP) associates.  

Despite this scale, last-mile delivery remains Amazon's single largest operational cost center, estimated at $14 to $17 per residential package, and its most complex risk domain. The fundamental challenge is optimization: with millions of daily stops distributed across diverse urban, suburban, and rural geographies. Amazon's current route optimization infrastructure, while sophisticated, was built incrementally over two decades and lacks the unified AI-driven optimization intelligence required to remain competitive against a new generation of last-mile technology disruptors. For this reason, Amazon is considering acquiring an AI-powered route optimization service that can improve routing decisions, reduce delivery delays, lower costs, and make delivery operations more efficient.
